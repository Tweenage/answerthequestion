The Hidden Problem

Most 11+ marks aren't lost through lack of knowledge. They're lost through lack of focus.

Tricky wording, hidden steps, and cleverly designed distractors catch children out — even the brightest ones. The 11+ doesn't just test what your child knows. It tests whether they can stay calm, read carefully, and avoid the traps.

💡

Misreading the question

Words like "not", "never", "only", and "always" reverse or limit what's being asked. Under pressure, children skip past them — and choose the opposite answer.

⏱

Rushing under time pressure

Anxiety makes children race through questions. They grab the first plausible answer instead of reading all options and eliminating wrong ones methodically.

🧠

Cognitive overload

Multi-step maths problems, complex verbal reasoning codes, and dense comprehension passages overwhelm working memory — causing careless mistakes on questions they could solve.

🎯

Falling for clever distractors

11+ exams are designed with plausible-sounding wrong answers. Children pick options that "feel right" without proving them against the text or checking their working.

All 11+ Subjects

One topic per day. Every trap covered.

Each day focuses on a single subject, keeping sessions fresh and building well-rounded exam technique across everything the 11+ throws at them.

📚

English Comprehension

Literal and inferential understanding, vocabulary in context, and multi-answer questions.

Common trap: Choosing answers that reuse passage phrases without fully answering the question.
🔢

Maths

Multi-step word problems, data interpretation, fractions, percentages, and unit conversions.

Common trap: Missing hidden operations — "each" implies multiplication, "remaining" implies subtraction.
🔬

Verbal Reasoning

Analogies, letter codes, synonyms/antonyms, word puzzles, and multi-answer word clues.

Common trap: Picking the first plausible answer without checking it against all the clue conditions.
🔷

Non-Verbal Reasoning

Pattern sequences, rotations, reflections, odd-one-out, and embedded shapes.

Common trap: Confusing mirror images — misidentifying which axis the reflection uses.
